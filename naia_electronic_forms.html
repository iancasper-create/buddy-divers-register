Diver Medical | Participant Questionnaire

DAN  |  WRSTC  |  RSTC  |  Undersea & Hyperbaric Medical Society  |  CMAS

Recreational scuba diving and freediving requires good physical and mental health. There are a few medical conditions which can be hazardous while diving, listed below. Those who have, or are predisposed to, any of these conditions, should be evaluated by a physician. This Diver Medical Participant Questionnaire provides a basis to determine if you should seek out that evaluation. If you have any concerns about your diving fitness not represented on this form, consult with your physician before diving. If you are feeling ill, avoid diving. If you think you may have a contagious disease, protect yourself and others by not participating in dive training and/ or dive activities. References to “diving” on this form encompass both recreational scuba diving and freediving. For your safety, and that of others who may dive with you, answer all questions honestly.

Nai'a Named Storm Policy

A week or ten days diving in the South Pacific is a rare opportunity for most people, often planned years in advance. Unfortunately, tropical cyclones occasionally occur during the summer and, although we have only lost a total of 12 days since 1993 its horrible if the storm happens to choose your week in paradise. NAI'A will compensate the guests for diving days lost from their charter due to a named storm.

Any guest whose diving is interrupted as a result of a named storm will receive a Named Storm credit voucher based on the number of diving days missed. If a charter has to be cancelled in advance, started late or ended early due to an approaching named storm, guests will receive a voucher for the total number of days credited. The “Named Storm” vouchers allow guests to use those days towards another trip within two years of their interrupted charter.

Our Named Storm program is not a substitute for the purchase of a comprehensive Trip Cancellation and Interruption Insurance policy. Please Note: Trip Cancellation and Interruption Insurance cannot be purchased once a storm has been named and Named Storm vouchers apply to passengers who do not receive compensation from their trip insurance carrier. The Named Storm program is specifically for lost days on a charter and is not issued when guests are unable to get to the destination due to transportation or other issues, storm-related or not.

NAI'A has a comprehensive if seldom enacted storm plan in case of such events. If weather forecasts or conditions appear threatening to the safety of the passengers and crew, we will make necessary arrangements to evacuate the guests. The captain will order and arrange for guests to depart NAI'A with a crew member, and be taken to a resort, hotel or government approved shelter. In most cases we can get guests back to one of the major hotels near the international airport. NAI'A will then be moved to our cyclone mooring where she will be prepared and secured by the remaining crew.
